Research
Noise-Adversarial Learning by Preserving Structural Stability for Text-Based Person Search
Zimo Li, Liam
NeurIPS, 2025
[Paper] / [Code]

In the field of TBPS, the datasets used for training are usually manually annotated. Although great efforts have been made to annotate these data, it is inevitable that there will be some noisy matching pairs. If noisy matching pairs are ignored during training, the geometric structure of the network will be damaged, resulting in overfitting of the model. In this paper, we use the geometric structure that maintains stability to counter these noisy samples, thereby achieving good performance of the model.
